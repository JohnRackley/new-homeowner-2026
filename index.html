Step 3

Understand Your Insurance & Tax Exposure (Not Just the Payment)

One of the most common 2026 homeowner surprises is an escrow change. Taxes reassess, premiums adjust, and deductibles can be higher than people realize.

Insurance: confirm these 5 items

  • Dwelling coverage is based on replacement cost (not the purchase price).
  • Deductibles (especially wind/hail or named storm, where applicable).
  • Personal property coverage limits for your belongings.
  • Liability coverage amount that fits your situation.
  • Excluded perils (flood, earth movement, etc.).

Taxes: know what can change

  • Whether your area has reassessments after a sale.
  • Homestead / primary residence exemptions (if applicable).
  • How often the county updates values.
  • How to read an escrow analysis notice.
Quick check: Pull up your insurance declarations page and highlight the deductibles. If you can‚Äôt explain them in one sentence, call your agent and ask.
